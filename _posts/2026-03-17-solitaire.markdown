## In other news: I definitely, 100% do not have a solitaire addiction.

It started last year in April (How time flies, nearly one year of me grinding solitaire), when I would play solitaire during one of my general education classes. The professor didn’t care. We still had a long debate about Cormac McCarthy (I did not like The Road) several times so I was paying attention, just doing something with my hands.

Since then I make sure to complete my solitaire dailies. Not everyday, but definitely at least once a week I sit down for twenty to thirty minutes and grind out a week’s worth of solitaire. 

I always make sure to earn gold for the month, diamond if I feel inclined. I’ve never perfected a month, but it’s on my lofty list of life goals, somewhere between eating a potato donut and beating my father in chess.

## But why?

Why do I mention this? Why do I make a big deal out of it? Well, it’s a habit, a routine I have developed and maintained over the past few months. And the past few months have not been routine at all.

Between living on the other side of the planet for two months to now living in yet another different country, I have not maintained a life routine for where I have been living, and I think it is getting to me. Or the burn out, it always could be the burnout.

But this week I woke up and decided that I need a routine again. Well, really it was I was staring at the ceiling questioning all of my life decisions and realized I felt more put together in high school because I had a routine. It’s close enough.

In addition to enforcing bedtime and rising rules, I have officially given up all social media in favor of books (which will probably work as I am currently on a six book series, but we will see when it ends), made a dedicated working spot, and started to make sure I eat breakfast everyday.

And while these things might seem really small and meaningless to others, for me it’s a really big deal as I told myself multiple times this semester that tomorrow would be the change but on Monday I decided it was Monday and nothing was going to stop me.

And it’s **amazing.**

I must have forgotten how much willpower I have. The will to do something is so powerful, and I am ready to bring so much more will.

It makes me feel more productive, even if it’s just making my bed and breakfast. I am starting to feel like I am accurately playing an adult again, instead of just failing and impersonating an actor.

And I have more goals. I want to get back into my exercise routine, even if volleyball and martial arts are far away. I want to get back into playing music daily, as that helps a lot with my self expression. And eventually, I want to get back to writing and programming for myself.

One of the things school destroyed for me is when you major in what you love, you have to do a lot of soulless work. Luckily, I make video games for a living right now so it’s not terrible, but still extremely draining at times.

I want to get back to making silly, simple games where I can make the weirdest physics happen. I want to get back to writing my novel(s). I want to get back to working for me, making things that drive me, feeling rejuvenated from work in a way nothing else can.

## One last round...

There’s a saying that says habits take twenty-one days to form. But rather, after a quick Google search, they take at least twenty-one days and more often two months to solidify. Generally routines are fun the first week with the novelty, but then lull the next because of the brain resisting change. However, by the sixty-six day mark, there was often success in those who stuck through, and they felt happier for it.

So that means by the end of the semester I will have new habits formed. And that is exciting enough to hopefully keep me going, even when the novelty wears off. I did it once with solitaire, how hard can it be to do it again?
